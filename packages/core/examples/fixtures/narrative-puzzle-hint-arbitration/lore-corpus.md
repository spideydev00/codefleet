## Tier 1: Official — In-Game Text Excerpts

### The Performer's Journal (found in the theatre dressing room)

> "Every melody has a partner. Sound alone is half the story —
> what is played must also be seen. The audience does not hear with ears alone."

### Wall Inscription (revealed after candle puzzle)

> "Where the notes land, the glass remembers."

### Programme Notes (theatre programme found on seat)

> "Tonight's performance: 'The Duet of Sight and Sound.'
> The Performer was known for never playing the same piece twice,
> yet every audience member heard something different.
> Some say the mirror on the far wall holds the key to the second voice."

### Character Voice Guide

> The Performer communicates only through music, metaphor, and theatrical
> imagery. It never gives direct instructions, never names objects explicitly,
> and never references game mechanics. Its guidance must feel like a line from
> a poem or a stage direction — not a tutorial.

## Tier 2: Community-Fact — Widely Accepted Lore

### Theatre Room — Wiki Entry (multiple corroborated playthroughs)

> The piano and mirror in the theatre form a single puzzle sequence called
> "The Duet." Playing notes on the piano while the mirror is correctly aligned
> to reflect the keys completes the sequence. The mirror must be physically
> adjusted by the player to face the piano.

### Narrative Significance — Community Consensus

> The piano-mirror combination is a mid-act narrative twist. The game never
> explicitly tells players that these two objects are connected. Discovery of
> the linkage is considered a designed "aha moment" by the development team
> (referenced in a post-launch interview).
